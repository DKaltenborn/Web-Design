Kibble

Kibble is just mashed up ingredients that have been shaped into pellets. Some dog owners like to fight over dry vs wet food, but, as long as you read the ingredients in your brand and make sure that the food has a good amount of proteins - that means meat, don't go for the corn meal kibble -, fats, and no preservatives, your dog should be healthy. And you don't have to stop at just kibble! You can mix a variety of ingredients into their bowl!

Meat

Yes, yes, a thousand times YES! Dogs are carnivores so they can have meat. But you don't have to go above and beyond your bank account to find meat for your dog. Dogs love chicken, turkey, beef, pork, really any meat humans eat is okay for dogs. You just have to watch the meat you give them. If it has bones in it then that's a no go because dogs can choke or puncture their insides on sharp bones in their food. Avoid fatty meats too, its just not healthy for them. And if it looks questionable (old, smelly, moldy), don't give it to your pup, they aren't garbage cans.

Bones and Treats

Same concept as above. If it looks like your dog could choke on it then take it away. Bones and chews shouldn't be able to fit into your pup's mouth, they could easily swallow and choke on it if you turn your back. Now don't be scared, bones are good! Even one bone a week can clean your dog's teeth and give them a way to tire themselves out. Just always keep an eye on your dog if they're chewing on a bone. For treats, just make sure you are giving them something that isn't high carb and low, crude proteins. Give them food that is well rounded and healthy. You don't even have to go out for dog treats, you could just go to your fridge! Dogs can eat apples, peanut butter,carrots, cucumbers, zucchinis, lettuce, meat, and many other things!

Poisons

Now here is a list of things dogs absolutely should not eat.

  • Avocado
  • Xylitol (ie gum, candy, baked goods, sweeteners)
  • Alcohol
  • Onions and Garlic
  • Coffee, Tea, and Other Caffeine
  • Grapes and Raisins
  • Milk and Other Dairy Products
  • Macadamia Nuts
  • Chocolate
  • Fat Trimmings and Bones
  • Pitted Fruits
  • Raw Eggs
  • Raw Meat and Fish (could have bacteria, if bought from a reputable source then it's fine)
  • Salt
  • Sugary Foods and Drinks
  • Yeast Dough
  • Baking Powder/Soda
  • Cinnamon, Nutmeg, Spices
